Bio

I am a PhD Candidate at CICS, University of Massachusetts Amherst. My research examines learning problems with an emphasis on their foundations as well as applications. In particular, my research interests include Gaussian processes and NLP, with a special focus on, fast inference for Gaussian processes, and representation learning for NLP and information extraction.

Currently, I'm advised by Prof. Daniel Sheldon and actively collaborating with Prof. Cameron Musco. Previously, I worked with Prof. Andrew McCAllum and I was a research engineer and got my bachelors from Indian Institute of Technology, Mandi.
